He Is the Corporate Silence: Dread Him Not!

September 10, 1980
Jen Wilson

Residents and fans in a New York neighborhood are piping mad. They were all excited to rename the streets after the poet who had lived in a nearby farmhouse. Unfortunately, important details were missed. The blame can be passed to any and all above.

What is the mistake, you ask? The poet's name is Edgar Allan Poe. The street name, however, is Edgar Allen Poe. Residents are appalled and upset, and they demand rectification. The city has offered to glue "a"s over the "e"s, but most residents say that is not good enough.

No word yet on the outcome.
